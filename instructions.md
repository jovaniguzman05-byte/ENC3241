# Instructions

# How to Hone a Chef’s Knife Using a Honing Rod

## ⚠️ Safety Warning
Always keep your fingers away from the blade edge. Improper handling can cause serious injury.

## Overview
Honing a knife maintains edge alignment, improving cutting performance without removing significant material from the blade.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### 1. Stabilize the Honing Rod
Place the honing rod vertically on a stable surface.

### 2. Grip the Knife Properly
Hold the chef’s knife firmly by the handle using your dominant hand. Keep fingers clear of the blade.

### 3. Set the Angle
Position the blade at a **15–20° angle** against the rod.

![Knife Angle](images/knife-angle.jpg)
*Figure 1. Proper knife angle when honing*

### 4. Start at the Heel
Place the heel of the knife (thickest part near the handle) at the top of the rod.

![Knife Anatomy](images/knife-anatomy.png)
*Figure 2. Anatomy of a knife (heel, blade, tip)*

### 5. Execute the Stroke
Draw the blade downward along the rod toward the tip using light pressure in a smooth motion.

### 6. Alternate Sides
Repeat the same motion on the opposite side of the rod.

### 7. Repeat
Alternate sides for **8–10 strokes**.

### 8. Clean the Blade
Wipe the blade with a clean towel to remove residue.

![Clean Knife](images/clean-knife.jpg)
*Figure 3. Wipe blade with clean towel*

### 9. Test Sharpness
Test the knife on a soft ingredient (e.g., tomato). A properly honed blade should cut cleanly without slipping.

## Key Notes
- Honing ≠ sharpening (this maintains edge alignment, not grind removal)  
- Consistency of angle matters more than speed  
- Use light pressure—let the rod do the work
