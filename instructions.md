# Instructions

How to Hone a Chef’s Knife Using a Honing Rod

Warning: Always keep your fingers away from the blade edge. Improper handling of a knife can cause serious injury.

Steps 1: Place the honing rod on a stable surface.

Step 2: Hold the chef’s knife securely by the handle and use your dominant hand and keep your fingers clear of the blade.

Step 3: Position the knife at a 15–20-degree angle against the rod.
 
Figure 1. Proper knife angle when using a honing rod.

Step 4: Place the heel of the knife blade against the top of the rod.
 
Figure 3. Anatomy of a Knife

The heel is the thickest portion of the blade closest to the handle.

Step 5: Slide the blade downward along the rod toward the tip Apply light pressure while pulling the blade down in a smooth motion.

Step 6: Repeat the motion on the opposite side of the rod.

Step 7: Continue alternating sides for 8–10 strokes.

Step 8: Wipe the blade with a clean towel.
 
Figure 3. Properly wipe the knife with a clean towel.

Step 10: Test the knife on a soft ingredient such as tomato.
