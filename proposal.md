# Proposal

# Evaluating the Impact of Certified Angus Beef® on Restaurant Profitability and Customer Perception

**Author:** Jovani Guzman  
**Department:** Commerce Leadership B.A.

## Background

Restaurant operators consistently face margin pressure while trying to maintain quality. Many rely on commodity beef to control costs, often sacrificing consistency and guest satisfaction. Premium programs like Certified Angus Beef® (CAB) offer stricter specifications and more consistent marbling, which can improve the dining experience and justify higher menu pricing.

However, the core issue is execution, not just product cost. Operators often misjudge yield, portioning, and pricing strategy, leading to incorrect assumptions about profitability.

This study focuses on one targeted survey and one supporting cost analysis to evaluate whether CAB improves profitability and perceived value when executed correctly.

## Methodology

### Primary Research: Customer Survey

- **Sample:** 50–75 guests at 2 participating restaurants using CAB
- **Focus Areas:**
  - Perceived quality
  - Willingness to pay
  - Overall satisfaction

### Supporting Analysis

The study will analyze:

- Cost per pound
- Yield percentage
- Menu price versus plate margin

> **Note:** Operator interviews and multi-account comparisons were removed to tighten the scope and align with a one-semester funded project.

## Anticipated Outcomes

- Demonstrate whether CAB drives higher perceived value and willingness to pay
- Identify whether menu pricing and yield optimization offset higher food cost
- Deliver a practical profitability framework for operators using premium beef

## Dissemination Plan

Results will be shared through:

- A formal presentation to Buckhead Meat & Seafood leadership and sales teams
- A practical sales playbook for foodservice professionals
- A summary report distributed to participating restaurant operators
- Optional submission to a hospitality or foodservice academic forum

## Significance

This project has direct relevance to:

- Restaurant profitability decisions
- Sales strategy in center-of-the-plate categories
- Customer value perception in premium dining
